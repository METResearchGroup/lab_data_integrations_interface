## Compaction and deduplication

| Table | Files before | Files after | Rows before | Rows after | Redelivered dupes | Lifecycle collapses | Tombstones dropped | Bytes before | Bytes after |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| posts | 19 | 2 | 19,440 | 17,123 | 0 | 212 | 2,105 (10.8%) | 3.36 MiB | 3.01 MiB |
| likes | 11 | 2 | 86,742 | 84,950 | 0 | 623 | 1,169 (1.3%) | 11.08 MiB | 9.76 MiB |
| reposts | 10 | 1 | 13,666 | 13,057 | 0 | 77 | 532 (3.9%) | 1.85 MiB | 1.56 MiB |
| follows | 10 | 1 | 11,977 | 8,984 | 0 | 143 | 2,850 (23.8%) | 0.98 MiB | 0.81 MiB |

**Redelivered duplicates across all tables: 0.** A redelivered duplicate is the identical event twice -- same `uri` *and* same `cid`. Lifecycle collapses are different: several distinct events about one record (create then delete, create then update), each with its own `cid`. Only the first is a stream defect; the second is a record's history being materialised into current state.

Tombstones are `delete` rows, which carry no record body. They are dropped at compaction. Note this only cancels a create that is in the same table -- a delete of a record written before this table existed has nothing to reconcile against and is simply discarded.
